Team Organisation

We met at the beginning of each sprint. We would break down the milestones of the sprint into tasks, and would arrange ourselves into teams depending on the ongoing tasks. Using Trello, individuals were assigned those tasks. Most teams remained the same across various sprints and some teams were rearranged when needed.

At the end of each sprint, each team wrote a Nuclino page with instructions on how they accomplished their tasks and problems they faced, alongisde their solutions and likely causes. This is so if members of a team would become unavailable at some point in the future, other members of the group can build on top of what it's already built with the already-existing work fully documented.

Team meetings and meetings with the mentor took place on a weekly basis. To keep every member of the team up to date with other members' work, the team had daily scrums over Slack. The 'scrum window' would last for 2 hours. In this window, each member writes what they have done since the last scrum, what they are currently working on and what they'll work on next. Daily scrums allowed all members of the team to be aware of occuring problems immediately, instead of delaying problems to the weekly meetings.

Development tools used included git to manage the working files, which were maintained on a Github repository. Trello was also used to assign tasks and deadlines to each member at the beginning of each sprint. Slack was used for daily communication.
